## Domain and application areas

This section classifies papers by the primary domain or application addressed and highlights representative studies for each area. The list focuses on explicit domain statements in abstracts or titles from the provided corpus.

- **Recommender systems** Evidence from an empirical study on transparent exploration interfaces and a study on movie-recommendation explanations show research on explanation styles and exploration feedback design [1] [2].  
- **Social media and platform transparency** Co-design work and recommendation-reporting prototypes examine what platforms should disclose to users about algorithms [3].  
- **News personalization** A quantitative user study of a news-aggregator prototype investigated user control and importance of algorithmic information with 432 respondents [4].  
- **Intelligent vehicles** User studies evaluated explainability for in-vehicle adaptive comfort and infotainment features and report effects on perceived understandability and acceptance [5].  
- **Forecasting support systems** Literature review and interface designs test degrees of transparency in forecasting tools and their effect on manual adjustments [6].  
- **Healthcare and medical decision support** An online empirical study examined the influence of algorithm reliability and transparency on decisions about melanoma probability [7].  
- **Loan and consequential decisions** Large lab experiments and frameworks study explanations in loan decisions to measure understanding, trust, and usage intention [8].  
- **Public administration and street‑level decision‑making** Experimental scenarios (e.g., visa denials, welfare fraud) test how accessibility and explainability affect perceived trustworthiness [9].  
- **Video summarization and multimedia** User studies assessed visual explanation types for transparency in video summaries and their utility for archivists and curators [10].  
- **General XAI method evaluation** Comparative human-centered evaluations measure interpretability and user task performance across explanation methods for image classification and other tasks [11].

## Research methodologies used

This section summarizes the main research methods in the collection and connects each method to exemplar papers; it highlights that empirical user-focused methods dominate the set. The first paragraph ties the methodological mix to the question of how transparency is studied.

- **Lab experiments** Controlled lab studies measuring perceptions of explanations and decision outcomes are used in large-scale experiments on explanation characteristics and loan decisions [8] [12].  
- **Online user studies** Mechanical Turk and other online within-subject studies are used for interface comparisons and exploration feedback elicitation (e.g., 94 MTurk workers) [1].  
- **Surveys and questionnaires** Non-experimental quantitative questionnaires assessed user preferences and control requirements for news aggregator personalization (n=432) [4].  
- **Within-subject and mixed‑methods designs** Several investigations combine quantitative measures with qualitative interviews to explain paradoxical effects of transparency [13] [12].  
- **Comparative method evaluations** Human-centered comparisons of multiple explanation techniques assess interpretability and task performance across methods [11] [14].  
- **Literature reviews and theoretical frameworks** Some works synthesize prior findings or propose frameworks linking philosophy, psychology, and ML interpretability to define good explanations [6] [15].

## Evaluation metrics and outcome measures

This section lists common outcome variables used across studies and ties each metric to exemplar papers that used or discussed it. The opening relates these metrics to how transparency effects are operationalized.

Commonly used evaluation metrics and measures
- **Trust and trustworthiness** Frequently measured as perceived trust in algorithmic decisions or in human decision-makers interacting with algorithms [9] [8].  
- **Perceived transparency / understandability** Studies directly measure user judgments about how transparent or understandable the system/explanation is [5] [1].  
- **Accuracy perception and acceptance** Users’ perceptions of system accuracy and their acceptance or willingness to use the system are common outcomes [12] [1].  
- **Satisfaction and usability** Satisfaction, preference, and perceived usefulness appear in recommender and explanation-style comparisons [1] [2].  
- **Feedback elicitation and engagement** Number of feedback actions or engagement with exploratory items was an outcome in recommender interface work [1].  
- **Fairness and accountability perceptions** Studies in consequential domains probe user judgments about fairness, accountability, and appropriateness of decisions [8] [17].  
- **Behavioral outcomes and adjustability** Measured effects on user adjustment behavior and avoidance/aversion in response to transparency or adjustable predictions [19].  
- **Task performance and interpretability in human tasks** Measured via users’ ability to categorize or reason about model outputs when given different explanation methods [11] [14].

Each metric above is illustrated in the cited studies and reflects how empirical work operationalizes transparency and explainability outcomes [1] [2] [5] [8] [9] [11] [14] [19] [12].
